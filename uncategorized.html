Type 2 doesn't have to be a "progressive disease"

Your doctor may have told you that even with good control, your condition will deteriorate over time. Things don't have to be this way.

Maintaining good, solid control over your blood sugar can slow down, stop and even prevent diabetic complications.

Why Exercise?

Regular exercise improves your body's ability to process and use glucose (sugar) by decreasing insulin resistance. Muscles use glucose too. Bigger, stronger muscles absorb more glucose. Especially while you work out.
Uncategorized 12/18/16

Don't order scrambled eggs

A lot of restaurants, possibly most of them, use pre-scrambled eggs from cartons. These have additives and may include additional carbs.

If you want to get the real thing, order another style. Fried, poached, etc.

Diabetes driver's license laws by U.S. state

All states have special driver's license rules regarding medical conditions. Some states apply their rules to all diabetics. Others have rules only for those with specific conditions, such as neuropathy and hypoglycemia.

Alcohol and Insulin is a Tricky Mix. Be careful.

Drinking alcohol can stop or slow your liver in regards to dumping glucose.

Cut back a little on your insulin, bring hard candy for lows and consider having mixed drinks with regular soda.

Post Workout Spike

High blood sugars are common after workouts, especially when you're just getting into the swing of things.

Try eating a low-carb protein bar before you work out and see if that helps.
