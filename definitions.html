    HbA1c (also called A1c)

    A blood test that estimates average blood glucose level (BG) over the past 2 to 3 months.

    Diagnosis (ADA criteria) - Normal: under 5.7% - Pre-Diabetes: 5.7 to 6.5% - Diabetes: 6.5% and up

    Definitions 12/18/16

    Highs and Lows

    Both too much and too little sugar in the bloodstream can cause serious, even life threatening diabetic complications.

    Too little sugar in the blood is referred to as "hypoglycemia." Too much is called "Hyperglycemia."
    Definitions 11/13/16

    Hypoglycemia

    A condition in which there's not enough sugar in the blood for the body to function normally.

    When a person with diabetes passes out, it's often due to low blood sugar. It is an emergency. Dial 911. DO NOT GIVE INSULIN.

    Prevention

    Definitions 11/11/16

    Hyperglycemia

    Hypers, or Highs, refer to a high blood sugar levels. BG levels that are very high may be a symptom of ketoacidosis.

    Highs are often caused by missed or a miscalculated insulin dose, or eating too many carbs.

    High BG Symptoms

    Definitions 11/11/16

    What is diabetes?

    Diabetes is a condition in which the body doesn't properly process sugar for use as energy.

    Either the body does not produce insulin at all, or doesn't produce enough for the body to use sugar for energy as it should.

    Definitions 11/11/16

    Hypo Unawareness

    Most of the time people with diabetes can easily feel a low blood sugar event coming on. But that's not always the case. Being hypo unaware means your body may not feel the symptoms.

    Not knowing when you're going low can be dangerous.

    Definitions 11/11/16

    Chronic vs. Acute

    A "chronic" condition is one that is long lasting or reoccurring. For instance, heart disease is often a chronic condition.

    An "acute" condition is short lasting. The common cold, for example, is an acute condition.

    Definitions 11/11/16

    What is retinopathy?

    Diabetic retinopathy is a serious, sight-threatening complication of diabetes.

    Over time, high blood sugars damage the blood vessels in the retina, causing the tissue to swell. This causes cloudy or blurred vision, or even blindness, in both eyes.

    Definitions 11/11/16

    Type 1 vs. Type 2

    In T1, the pancreas doesn't produce insulin. Insulin must be injected.

    T2 begins with "insulin resistance." The body can produce insulin but does a poor job of using it. Many T2's, not all, need to inject insulin.

    Types of diabetes

    Definitions 11/06/16

    Ketoacidosis (it's bad news)

    When the body can't process carbohydrates, it starts using fat for fuel. This builds up a high level of "ketones" in the blood. Ketones are acidic.

    This is a potentially deadly situation and may require immediate hospitalization.

    Causes/Symptoms

    Definitions 11/06/16

    Hyperinsulinemia

    In response to high levels of blood sugar in T2, the pancreas sends out more and more insulin. This condition is called Hyperinsulinemia and it's bad for your heart.

    Taking insulin shots does not cause this problem, and in fact may help prevent or alleviate the issue.
    Definitions 11/06/16

    Basal vs. Bolus

    Long acting insulin, such as Lantus, is referred to as Basal Insulin. The purpose is to set a consistent baseline of insulin in your system.

    Quick acting (meal time) insulin such as Novolog is referred to as Bolus Insulin. It's used to manage blood sugar spikes and meals.
    Definitions 11/05/16

    What is Neuropathy

    An estimated 20 million people in the US. have some form of neuropathy, which is defined as nerve disease or damage.

    Strict control of BG levels does reduce neuropathic symptoms and helps prevent further nerve damage.

    Definitions 11/05/16

    Ketosis

    When the body burns fat for energy instead of carbohydrates, it creates "ketones." When it does so, your body is in "ketosis."

    This may mean you're doing a good job eating low-carb and losing weight. It can also be a sign of ketoacidosis (which is very bad).
    Definitions 11/05/16

    Dawn Phenomenon (DP)

    When waking up in the morning, the liver kicks sugar into your bloodstream to give you a little boost. This is a common cause of high morning BG's.

    Tip: try eating a small snack before bed, or drinking a shot of alcohol.

    Definitions 10/27/16

    Diabetic Complications

    Diseases and significant health issues caused by ongoing high BG levels. For example: blindness, kidney disease and loss of limb.

    Maintaining normal blood sugar levels can prevent, slow and possibly reverse complications.
