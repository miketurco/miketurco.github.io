Read syringes the easy way

Use a lighted magnifying glass so that the small numbers are easier to see.

Get the right syringes: 30 unit syringes have half-unit markings; 50 unit syringes go by ones and 100 unit syringes go by twos.

Tips for daily foot care

With diabetes, you may not have much feeling in your feet. You should look at them, dialy, to make sure they're OK.

Here's the easy way: Sit in a chair. Use a mirror, footstool, and a small lamp on a stand.

Small improvements in blood sugar reduce risk

The closer your blood sugar is to normal, the better your chance of avoiding serious complications. Modest, sustained improvements to BG levels can prevent kidney failure, amputations and heart failure.

High fasting BG? (Somogyi)

This could be the "somogyi effect" which is a rebound from a low. Possibly from too much basal insulin.

Nighttime lows can be very dangerous. Ask your doctor or educator how to "Basal Test."

Preventing Lows

Carb and insulin calculations can be a little off in the "real world."

Stick to the safe side. Let your BG run a little high when it comes to meals of unknown carb content. You can correct later if need be.

Why A1c tests are important

A1c tests, more-or-less, provide your average blood sugar for the last two to three months. Knowing your average blood sugar helps you keep an eye on the big picture. Especially for those who don't test throughout each day.

Wash hands before testing

Washing your hands before testing does make a difference.

Studies have shown that oils and food residue on your hands can raise your readings by two to three points.

How to "eat to your meter"

See how specific foods and meals affect your BG. Measure right before you start to eat. Then measure again at one and two hours.

Note that some foods may take longer to affect Your blood sugar levels.

Tips 11/07/16

Candy tip (for lows)

When your sugar drops, your hands can get shaky and sometimes the mind clouds.

This is not the time to deal with a hard to open package. Keep your candy in a bowl or loosely closed jar.
Tips 11/06/16

Get the right size lancets for you

Try different size lancets. The thinnest are 32 gauge and the thickest are 28.

Thicker lancets sound like they would hurt more, but you may not have to poke as deep to draw the same amount of blood.
Tips 11/05/16

Fingertip Testing: First Drop?

First or second drop? If you wash your hands before testing, use the first. If your hands are soiled or have food on them, wipe off the first drop use the second one.
Tips 11/05/16

Drip, don't squeeze

Squeezing the blood out of your finger can mix fluid from between your skin cells into the sample.

Bringing this "interstitial fluid" into the mix can raise your BG reading by a few points.

Sleep Problems Tied to Type 2 Diabetes in women

Based on a ten-year study on 130,000+ women. Scientists looked difficulty falling or staying asleep, frequent snoring, apnea and rotating shift work. Results: self-reported sleep difficulty increases diabetes risk by 22%.
