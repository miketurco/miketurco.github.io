Either you have diabetes or you don't. The term "prediabetes" does, however, tell you where you're at in terms of this disease. Learn more.

Most who have it diabetes don't know

According to the CDC, 90% of Americans with Pre-D don't know they have it. It takes a doctor to diagnose diabetes. However, you can keep an eye out for warning signs yourself. A1c test kits and blood glucose meters are available at Walmart.

Vit D supps don't prevent diabetes

Vitamin D reduces the risk of insulin resistance, which leads to T2 diabetes.

Pre-D's who deficient in Vitamin D are more likely to develop full-blown diabetes. Unfortunately, taking supplements doesn't help.
