Diabetic Peripheral Neuropathy

Nerve damage caused by long-term high blood sugar levels. It affects the legs, feet, hands and arms.

Symptoms: a tingling or a burning sensation, shooting pains, loss of balance and wounds that don't heal.

How to prevent (and treat) toenail fungus

Toenail fungus loves high blood sugar, which is why it's such a common problem for diabetics.

Both prevention and cure depend on good blood sugar control and hygiene. Medications can help as well.

Types of diabetic eye complications

People with diabetes are at a higher risk of blindness. Most, however, experience nothing more than minor eye disorders.

Regular checkups + knowing what to look for helps keep small problems in check.

Tendon pain now a type 2 complication

People with type 2 are more than three times as likely to have tendon pain, known as tendinopathy. Also, those diagnosed with tendinopathy should be tested for diabetes.

Diabetic Nephropathy

Kidney disease or kidney damage often occurs over time in people with diabetes. This type of kidney disease is called diabetic nephropathy.

High blood sugar, high blood pressure and smoking are common causes.

What hyperinsulinemia is and why it matters

Hyperinsulinemia is the condition in which your body is producing way too much insulin. This is a very common problem in T2 diabetes, and is a causative factor in heart disease. Unfortunately, most doctors do not test for this condition.
