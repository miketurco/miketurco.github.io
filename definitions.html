Diabetes and diabetes related definitions

This site depends on your support

Understanding what diabetes is and how to describe the various aspects of it is half the battle. Here's where to find diabetes words and phrases that matter, plus others that are good to know.

Diabetic Complications

Diseases caused by ongoing high BG levels. Including as blindness, kidney disease and loss of limb.

Maintaining normal blood sugar levels can prevent, slow down and possibly reverse complications.

Definitions 12/24/16

What is Neuropathy

An estimated 20 million people in the US. Have neuropathy, which is defined as nerve disease or damage.

Strict control of BG levels reduces neuropathic symptoms and can prevent further nerve damage.

Definitions 12/24/16

Hypoglycemia

A condition in which there's not enough sugar in the blood for the body to function normally.

When a person with diabetes passes out, it's often due to low blood sugar. Dial 911. DO NOT GIVE INSULIN.

Definitions 12/23/16

Hyperinsulinemia

In T2, the pancreas sends out more and more insulin to cover high BG's. This condition is called Hyperinsulinemia and it's bad for your heart.

Taking insulin shots does not cause this problem, and in fact may help prevent the problem.
Definitions 12/23/16

Type 1 vs. Type 2

In T1, the pancreas doesn't produce insulin. Insulin must be injected.

T2 begins with "insulin resistance." The body can produce insulin but does a poor job of using it. Many T2's take insulin too.

Definitions 12/23/16

What is retinopathy?

Diabetic retinopathy is a sight-threatening diabetic complication.

Over time, high blood sugars damages the blood vessels in the retina. This causes cloudy or blurred vision, or even blindness, in both eyes.

Definitions 12/23/16

Hyperglycemia

Hypers, or Highs, refer to high blood sugar levels. Very high BG's may be a symptom of ketoacidosis.

Highs can be caused by a missed or a miscalculated insulin dose, or eating too many carbs.

Definitions 12/23/16

Chronic vs. Acute

A "chronic" condition is long lasting or reoccurring. For instance, heart disease is often a chronic condition.

An "acute" condition is short lasting. The common cold, for example, is an acute condition.

Definitions 12/23/16

Hypo Unawareness

Most people can feel a low blood sugar event coming on. Others are hypo unaware, which means their body may not feel the symptoms.

Not knowing when you're low can be dangerous.

Definitions 12/23/16

What is diabetes?

Diabetes is a condition in which the body doesn't properly process sugar for use as energy.

Either the body does not produce insulin at all, or not enough for the body to use sugar for energy as it should.

Definitions 12/23/16

HbA1c (also called A1c)

A blood test that estimates average blood glucose level (BG) over the past 2 to 3 months.

Diagnosis (ADA criteria) - Normal: under 5.7% - Pre-Diabetes: 5.7 to 6.5% - Diabetes: 6.5% and up

Definitions 12/18/16

Highs and Lows

Both too much and too little sugar in the bloodstream can cause serious, even life threatening diabetic complications.

Too little sugar in the blood is referred to as "hypoglycemia." Too much is called "Hyperglycemia."
Definitions 11/13/16

Pharmacokinetics

Describes how a drug moves into, through and out of the body.

This branch of science also describes how a medication is absorbed and distributed as it makes its way through your system.

Definitions 11/11/16

Ketoacidosis (it's bad news)

When the body can't process carbohydrates, it starts using fat for fuel. This builds up a high level of "ketones" in the blood. Ketones are acidic.

This is a potentially deadly situation and may require immediate hospitalization.

Definitions 11/06/16

Basal vs. Bolus

Long acting insulin, such as Lantus, is referred to as Basal Insulin. The purpose is to set a consistent baseline of insulin in your system.

Quick acting (meal time) insulin such as Novolog is referred to as Bolus Insulin. It's used to manage blood sugar spikes and meals.
Definitions 11/05/16

Ketosis

When the body burns fat for energy instead of carbohydrates, it creates "ketones." When it does so, your body is in "ketosis."

This may mean you're doing a good job eating low-carb and losing weight. It can also be a sign of ketoacidosis (which is very bad).
